Abstract    :
              This program provides funds to math, engineering, and computer science students
              for one and two year scholarships. This enables the students to complete their
              academic programs in a more timely manner. This program also includes industry
              mentors for each student, one-on-one faculty advisors, second year industry-
              based cooperative education and service learning opportunities, professional
              development activities, and strong student support services. This scholarship
              program provides broad opportunities that enable the participation of talented,
              but low-income students, underrepresented minorities, and person with
              disabilities the opportunity to complete an associate degree and to enter the
              workforce in this area of national need.
